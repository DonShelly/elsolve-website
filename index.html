§ 01

What we believe.

i

AI is most valuable when it behaves like infrastructure — running underneath the work, compounding quietly, not demanding attention of its own.

ii

Great systems begin with the business, not the model. We start from how a company actually runs, then find the leverage, then build.

iii

Owners should gain clarity, consistency, and decision leverage — not a second job managing tools. If a system adds overhead, it has failed.

iv

We ship. Strategy that never reaches production is a deck. We design, build, and hand over systems that keep working after we’ve gone.

§ 02

How we work.

01

Listen

We start with the business, not the tooling. One or two sessions to understand what you do, where the friction lives, and which decisions cost you the most time.

02

Map

We map the workflows, identify leverage points, and propose a small number of interventions — ranked by effort, risk, and expected return.

03

Build

We design and build the system — bespoke agents, workflow automations, or research and decision-support tools — fitted to your business, not a template.

04

Hand over

You own the system. We document, onboard your team, and remain available for optimisation and new use cases as your operation matures.

Most of our clients don’t want to become AI experts. They want an operator-grade system that makes their week calmer and their decisions sharper. That is the whole brief.

The ELSOLVE brief, in one sentence.

§ 04

Who we work with.

We work best with founders, consultants, and established business owners who know AI matters but don’t want to spend months becoming technical operators themselves. Many of the people we help are experienced leaders who want the upside of AI without the chaos, jargon, or wasted experimentation.

Our engagements tend to land in four shapes: solopreneurs building leverage, early-stage startups building their operating spine, consultancies embedding AI into the service they already sell, and established SMBs tidying up the repetitive edges of the business.

Early-stage startups

Consultancies

Established SMBs
Quiet, compounding wins on the operational edges.
